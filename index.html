Emilio Ocelotl

Mexico City, Mexico (1989). Composer and researcher. He studied Sociology and Music Technology at the National Autonomous University of Mexico. Its activity focuses on the practice and research of computer music, electroacoustic music and software studies. He has participated in artistic residences, tours, festivals, programs, seminars, conferences, symposia and meetings such as: International Live Coding Conference (Morelia, Mexico and Madrid, Spain), Flight Practices Program of the CMMAS (Morelia, Mexico), International Code and Music Symposium / * vivo * /, New Manuel Enriquez International Music Forum, Cervantino International Festival, Fonoteca (Mexico City, Mexico), Linux Audio Conference (Stanford, California), MediaLab-Prado (Madrid Spain), Hangar (Barcelona, Spain), Panke gallery (Berlin, Germany), International Symposium on Electronic Art ISEA (Manizales, Colombia), Databit.me Festival (Arles, France), Academy of Arts (Bern, Switzerland), Sound and Interactive Art Festival (Madrid, Spain), Platohedro and ITM (Medellín, Colombia) and Algorave (Leeds, Newcastle, Hull and London, United Kingdom). He is a member of LiveCodeNet Ensemble and RGGTRN.

R G G T R N

RGGTRN (pronounced reɣɣaeˈtɾon), originally an electroacoustic and mixed music duo founded in 2012 in Mexico City, has evolved into a collective that engages in algorithmic dance music and audiovisual improvisation informed by the Latinx context that revolves around its members.
